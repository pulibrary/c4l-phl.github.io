The City of Brotherly Love

Philadelphia is home to uncountable universities, museums, and cultural institutions. So many of us are a part of the code4lib community — we're excited to take our turn in hosting the national code4lib conference!

Financial Sketch

Based on the Raleigh hosting team's experience, we plan to engage a conference planning organization to negotiate all of our contracts, including venue, hotel, wireless, catering, and AV. We therefore have a projected budget based on previous years, but we do not have exact quotes. We expect:

  • Registration fee between $200-250
  • Hotel rates around $175
  • 400-500 attendees

We are committed to strong fundraising goals and controlling costs.

Ideas: New and Mature

  • Scavenger hunt-style preconferences! With so many universities and cultural institutions in Philadelphia proper, we would love to hold preconferences in different locations around the city.
  • We feel the conference is ready for multi-track. We'd like to try it for a half-day. Specific tracks will be determined based on presentations selected.

Calendar

A target range of dates has been narrowed down to February through March.

Transportation

Philadelphia has great links from all over the country.

  • The Philadelphia International Airport (PHL) is just a 20 minute train ride from Center City and is served by lots of airport shuttles.
  • Amtrak to Philly goes up and down the East cost with good connections from the midwest.
  • Great public transit (SEPTA) and walkability, even in February or March.

The city

Philadelphia is a city well known for its importance in US history, but it also boasts a vibrant cultural, arts, music and restaurant scenes. The New York Times ranked Philly as the #3 place in the world to visit. And Philly packs all of this into a small, easily navigable City Centre.

Entertainment

Several hundred cultural heritage organizations call the Philly area home including:

Amazing Historic Libraries

Restaurants

Philly's Food scene has exploded in the past decade with so many great options. Soul food, tapas, BYO, farm-to-table, renowned chefs, vegetarian / vegan, and more. Reading terminal market is a great iowntown lunch destination.
